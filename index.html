al Dente "to the teeth"

"Al Dente" is a way of cooking pasta to the correct texture to allow for in sauce cooking or to prevent over cooking at a later time. Although not always the texture preferred to everyone, if you boil the pasta to this state then continue to cook your pasta in the sauce, baking pasta, or preparing ahead for later heating it will keep it from being over cooked.

Battonet

"Battonet" is a thick cut commonly called "french fry cut" it literally translates to "small stick". It is a cut that has the dimensions 1/4 inch x 1/4 in x 2 inches roughly the size of a small pinky finger. Although it has many application I have mostly used this cutting vegetables for snacking, fries and roasted vegetables.

Coring

"Coring" is the process of removing the seeds or center portion of an item. Depending on what the item is it can be fairly simple or rather difficult, if thinking "I don't know how to do this" look it up, avocado related injuries sent an average of 24 people to the hospital a day in 2021. It is usually done to allow an item to be cut easier (ie - mangoes, avocado), remove distasteful portions (ie - Apples, pears) or to stuff with other items (ie - stuffed zucchini/cucumber).

Deep Fry

Deep Frying is the act of completely submerging an item in very hot oil. It creates a hard shell around the inside trapping the center while heating it through saturating the outer layers with fat. It can be done in a deep deep fryer or in a high sided pot at home, although if attempting to try at home be sure to look up the best safety practices and have a fire extinguisher nearby.

Espagnole Sauce

Espagnole Sauce is the basic technique (mother sauce) for making all brown gravies, if you want the best possible Thanksgiving gravy this is how you do it. Using a dark stock (made by roasting the bones) and a dark roux (made by patience) the combination and added vegetables creates a gravy with depth and complexity

Fillet

Fillet can either mean a cut of meat from the loin area or the process of removing the meat from the bones. Learning how to properly debone any meat or fish will allow you to work with mor flavorful and more variety of cuts.

Grilling (or Broiling)

Grilling is to cook food on a rack above or below direct radiant dry heat. The high heat seals in juices and provides additional flavors. THe rack should be preheated and oiled before food is added to ensure less stick and a better mark

Hollandaise

Hollandaise sauce (also known as aioli) is the basic technique for all egg yolk/emulsion thickened sauces. It uses fat then has egg yolk whipped into it until it becomes thick. Mayonnaise is an example of a Hollandaise style thickened 'sauce'

Incise

Incising commonly referred to as scoring is the act of cutting shallow slices across an item for the sake of tenderizing or allowing the marinade/herbs to penetrate the item easier. It also results in a greater surface area exposed to the heat, leading to more even cooking

Julienne

Julienne more commonly known as matchstick is a cut with the dimensions of 1/8 inch x 1/8 inch x 2 inches. It is most commonly used in salads and stir fries.

Back to Top

Knead

To knead something is the act of repetitive folding it with your hands or a machine. Most commonly used in bread however in order to get a vegetarian meatloaf or meatball a certain amount of kneading is required for it to stick properly

Back to Top

Liaison

Liaison is the process of thickening a soup, stew or sauce whether by roux, starch, butter or cream. Egg yolks must be tempered with hot liquid before adding to the liquid in order to prevent curdling

Back to Top

Mince

Mince is to finely chop an item into the smallest possible size with a blade, this is different than a brunoise as a mince has no desired product shape. Also used to describe meat or vegetables run through a grinder with a blade plate.
